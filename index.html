BMI (Body Mass Index) is a numerical value that is used to estimate whether a person is underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese based on their height and weight. Here's how you can calculate your BMI: Multiply your weight in pounds by 703 Divide that number by your height in inches, squared For example, if you weigh 150 pounds and are 5 feet 7 inches (67 inches) tall, your BMI calculation would be: (150 * 703) / (67 * 67) = 21.9 Here's the BMI chart to determine your BMI category: Below 18.5: Underweight 18.5 to 24.9: Normal weight 25 to 29.9: Overweight 30 or above: Obese It's important to note that BMI is just an estimate, and it may not be an accurate indicator of body fat for everyone. For example, athletes or individuals with a lot of muscle may have a higher BMI but still be in good health. It's always best to speak with a healthcare professional to determine if your BMI is within a healthy range and if there are any underlying health concerns.
